=== FRONTMATTER REVIEW: title-as-claim-makes-overlap-between-notes-visible.md ===

Checks applied: 4

INFO:
- [Description discrimination] The first clause ("When note titles are claims, overlap between notes is visible at the index level") closely restates the title, adding only "at the index level." The second clause ("topical titles hide overlap behind different labels for the same territory") adds a useful contrast. However, the body's strongest discriminator — that claim titles turn overlap detection from a reading task (expensive) into a scanning task (cheap) — is absent from the description. Surfacing that mechanism would sharpen retrieval value.
  Recommendation: Consider leading with the scanning-vs-reading cost shift, e.g. "Claim titles turn overlap detection into a scanning task — similar assertions are obvious in an index without opening files; topical titles hide duplication behind different labels."

CLEAN:
- [Title composability] "since title as claim makes overlap between notes visible, we designed..." reads naturally as a sentence fragment. No awkward grammar when linked.
- [Claim strength] The claim is specific and contestable — someone could argue that topical titles with good descriptions also expose overlap, or that claim titles generate false-positive overlap signals when adjacent claims are distinct but similar in phrasing. Not a truism.
- [Title-body alignment] The body directly supports the title's claim: it shows how claim titles make overlap visible at the index level with concrete examples, argues the scanning-vs-reading cost reduction, and explains how the benefit compounds with index size. No drift.
