=== FRONTMATTER REVIEW: the-boundary-of-automation-is-the-boundary-of-verification.md ===

Checks applied: 4

INFO:
- [Title-body alignment] The title asserts "the boundary" (singular, definitive), but the body's final caveat explicitly concedes: "the argument defends verification as *the primary structural* boundary, not the only one." The body also identifies error-cost tolerance and regulatory/trust constraints as separate factors. This is honest nuance rather than contradiction, and the strong-claim-with-nuanced-body pattern is standard for claim titles, but a reader encountering only the title could over-read the exclusivity.
  Recommendation: Consider whether the title should read "the primary boundary" or whether the current strong form is intentional rhetorical choice. If intentional, no change needed — the caveats do the work.

CLEAN:
- [Description discrimination] The description adds method ("Synthesis"), names the three evidence lines (oracle theory, labor economics, frontier-lab capability predictions), notes "distinct reasoning paths," and specifies the mechanism ("verification cost as the primary structural determinant"). Against the title's bare claim, the description tells a searcher this note is a convergence argument across three domains — strong discrimination from other oracle-related notes.
- [Title composability] "since the boundary of automation is the boundary of verification" and "because the boundary of automation is the boundary of verification" both read as natural sentence fragments. The title composes well as a linkable clause.
- [Claim strength] The claim is specific and contestable. Someone could reasonably argue that the boundary of automation is raw capability, data availability, regulatory constraints, or cost of computation. The note's own caveats section names several of these counterarguments, confirming this is a genuine assertion rather than a truism.
