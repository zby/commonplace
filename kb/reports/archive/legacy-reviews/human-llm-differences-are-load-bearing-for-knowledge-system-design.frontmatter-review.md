=== FRONTMATTER REVIEW: human-llm-differences-are-load-bearing-for-knowledge-system-design.md ===

Checks applied: 4

INFO:
- [Title-body alignment] The body's concluding "methodological claim" section introduces a prescriptive principle ("evaluate each human convention individually, checking whether its specific arguments transfer") that goes beyond the descriptive claim in the title ("differences are load-bearing"). The title says the differences matter; the conclusion says what to do about them. This is a natural progression rather than drift -- the prescription follows from the descriptive claim -- but a reader loading only the title would not expect the note to also establish an operational principle. If the note matures beyond seedling, consider whether the title should signal the prescriptive content (e.g., "...so each convention needs individual evaluation") or whether the methodological claim should be extracted to its own note.

CLEAN:
- [Description discrimination] The description adds mechanism the title cannot carry: it names the two specific reasons (inheriting human-oriented materials, producing dual-audience documents) and reframes from "differences exist" to "first-class design concern rather than a generic disclaimer." An agent seeing five results about human-LLM differences would immediately know this note is about the inherited-materials and dual-audience problems specifically.
- [Title composability] "since human-LLM differences are load-bearing for knowledge system design, we evaluate each convention individually" reads as a natural sentence fragment. The title functions well as a linkable clause.
- [Claim strength] "Load-bearing" is a specific and contestable modifier -- it asserts structural dependence, not merely relevance. Someone could reasonably argue that LLMs are sufficiently human-like that human-oriented knowledge designs work without modification, or that the differences are too volatile to design around. The claim carries genuine information. (Also a seedling, so the bar is lower.)
