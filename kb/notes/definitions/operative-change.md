# Operative change

A change is **operative** when it affects the system's subsequent operation over the relevant horizon: it persists long enough to matter there, and it has a path — a consumer, a channel, and a force, in [behavioral authority](./behavioral-authority.md) terms — through which it actually reaches later behavior. It is the [self-improving system](./self-improving-system.md) definition's guard against changes that exist without consequence.

## Scope

Two conditions, both needed:

- **Persistence over the relevant horizon.** The horizon is declared, not fixed by the definition. Cross-episode persistence is the usual reading for learning systems, but a shorter horizon can be the relevant one: a plan revised mid-deployment that governs the rest of the deployment window is operative for that window. Operativity does **not** require permanence — many useful adaptations are temporary and still operative, and a change later rolled back was operative while it held.
- **A behavioral-authority path.** Something must consume the change, through some channel, with some force. A reviewed note nothing loads, an approved patch never merged, a generated validator nothing invokes — each persists and affects nothing.

## Exclusions

- **Transient compensation.** An adjustment consumed entirely within the moment that produced it — the eye adapting to the dark, a control output — determines nothing afterward.
- **Inert persistence.** An artifact that survives without a consumer is stored, not operative. Persistence alone is the cheap half.
- **Permanence as a requirement.** Demanding that a change last forever, or survive retraining, confuses operativity with immutability; the definition asks only that the change operate over the horizon that matters for the claim being made.

## Misuse Cases

- Calling a change operative because it was accepted or written down, without naming what consumes it.
- Denying operativity to a temporary adaptation that demonstrably steered behavior over its window.
- Reporting operativity without the horizon: "operative" with no stated horizon is as underspecified as an autonomy grade with no declared boundary.
