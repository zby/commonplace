# Reflective systems

Theory for when a system's own representations can change its behavior — and for the stronger conditions under which a methodology can extend itself. The thread's organizing comparison is the Gödel machine, which internalizes every function of self-change behind a proof gate; Commonplace sits in the same design space with the gate held by tests, validators, and a human maintainer inside the declared boundary — reflective today, not autonomous, its coverage graded across the forms its behavior lives in. The property is worth wanting because accepted improvements land in the self-representation and compound; the individual notes carry that argument, function by function. **Actionability, reflection, governed adaptation, closure, and proof-governed self-modification are distinct properties, not stages in a maturity ladder.**

Read in order: the two definitions fix the vocabulary, the middle three develop the change loop and its limits, the comparison locates the proof-governed corner of the design space, and the case note discharges the classification against this repository's observed history.
