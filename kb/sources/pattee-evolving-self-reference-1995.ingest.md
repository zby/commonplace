## Summary

Pattee distinguishes logical self-reference, material causal loops, and cognitive introspection before defining the form relevant to open-ended evolution: an autonomous closure between material dynamics governed by physical laws and symbolic constraints governed by syntactic rules. He calls that relation **semantic closure**, because selected symbolic aspects make law-determined material aspects functional (§1). The genotype/phenotype, hardware/software, and law/initial-condition distinctions are parallel instances, but the paper's claim is specifically about material organizations capable of open-ended evolution (§§1-3). Symbols are material structures whose functions depend on a self-referent organization, a population, use in construction and control, and an environment; neither physical-law description alone nor free-floating formalism captures that function (§§3-6).

## Inherited Vocabulary

### Exact terminology and definitions

- **Self-reference** has distinct logical, material, and cognitive senses; material self-reference may name causal loops, while cognitive self-reference includes introspection (§1).
- **Semantic closure:** self-reference with open-ended evolutionary potential is “an autonomous closure” between material dynamics (physical laws) and symbolic constraints (syntactic rules); the freely selected symbolic aspects make physical aspects functional (§1).
- **Matter-symbol complementarity:** the categories are distinct but complementary, exemplified by phenotype/genotype, hardware/software, and laws/initial conditions (§§1-4).
- **Symbol function:** not an intrinsic law-based property of a symbol vehicle; it is a selective survival property of populations using symbols for construction and control in an environment (§§3, 5).

### Necessary conditions versus illustrative features

Pattee presents matter-symbol complementarity and semantic closure as necessary conditions for open-ended evolution, not sufficient ones (§1). Material embodiment is necessary for symbolic behavior, but a complete material correlate is not sufficient to explain function (§§3-5). DNA, self-replicating automata, measurement devices, and computers illustrate the structure; they are not interchangeable realizations with identical boundary conditions.

### Assumed system boundary and people as components

The focal system is a living material organization capable of open-ended evolution, including symbol vehicles, interpreting/constructing machinery, material dynamics, and the environment/population relation that gives functions survival significance. Humans can instantiate the organism/cognitive end of this scope, but the paper does not treat a human operator as a detachable component of a technical system; its minimal argument is biological and organizational.

### Causal structure

Symbolic memory constrains material construction and control; material dynamics construct and sustain the mechanisms that read and copy symbols; natural selection assigns functional significance across organism-environment histories. The closure is reciprocal but asymmetric in description: physical laws determine dynamics, while selected constraints choose among law-permitted alternatives (§§1, 4-7, 9-11).

### Constraints for later Commonplace notes

Treat matter-symbol complementarity only as a structural analogy for complementary representational regimes. Do not call a Markdown/code relation **semantic closure** unless the biological conditions and material-symbol functions are actually argued. Distinguish a stored description from the material machinery that interprets it, and distinguish necessary closure from sufficient open-ended evolution. This paper does not decide whether Commonplace is reflective or reflexive.

## Limitations (our opinion)

This is a theoretical synthesis rather than a test of software or knowledge systems. Its strongest necessity claims concern living, evolving material organizations; applying them to Commonplace risks erasing the biology, embodiment, population selection, and construction mechanisms that do the explanatory work.
