# A `crux` type, distinct from the gap register

Source: imported from the [ChatGPT second opinion](./chatgpt-second-opinion.md).

A crux is *what would move the bottom line* — necessarily verdict-relative, since a "would change the assessment if…" field presumes an assessment. This distinguishes it from a [gap](./gap-register.md), which is *what is not known* (a neutral map-layer artifact).

Under the [two-layer stance](./personal-epistemology-stance.md) both belong, in different layers: gaps on the neutral map, cruxes attached to assessment artifacts. A crux carries its statement, why it matters, current evidence, possible resolutions, expected belief movement, and the cheapest next investigation — making the KB useful for deciding *what to investigate next*, not just what is known.
