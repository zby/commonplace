- **Cumulativity's environment-mediated exclusion** — survives contact in every group, including two instructive live cases: `write-agent-memory-system-review`'s replacement protocol is *deliberately* non-cumulative (the incumbent reaches the successor only through the environment, which the exclusion correctly declines to count — non-cumulativity as a chosen anti-anchoring feature), and the full-pass re-entrancy preflight is a genuine cumulativity wire (a later pass consumes an earlier pass's rejected disposition). Confirms F10.
- **Promotion vocabulary** — ~10 uses catalogued across the seven groups; all sit in the "move into a more binding or durable layer" family, with `demote`/"demotion" as the family's inverse. One watch item stands: skill promotion is channel-*creating* (it creates the consumer and channel), not merely durability-increasing — distinct enough that a cheap registration line under the write-time collision rule would be justified if the word's traffic grows. No collision today. Confirms F10.
- **Reflective-systems axis factoring — resolved 2026-07-23, no new tag.** Routing evidence from all seven groups: both reflection- and self-improvement-shaped traffic route through the single self-improving-systems head; no group observed reflection-shaped traffic misrouted by that head; the only consumer-visible seam was a dead end (the head's Related Tags claimed `computational-model` covers reflection and intercession while that head mentioned neither) — fixed with a reciprocal Related Tags line in `kb/notes/computational-model-README.md` rather than a new head. A minted `reflective-systems` head would be consumed by the same mechanisms (connect prospecting, tag sweep) at no extra wiring cost, but with no observed traffic to justify head maintenance, minting now would be structure ahead of evidence. Reopen if reflection-shaped queries are observed misrouted, or if the reflection cluster's routing traffic outgrows what co-occurrence on the existing tag expresses.
