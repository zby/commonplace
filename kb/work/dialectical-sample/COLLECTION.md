# Writing conventions for dialectical-sample (dialectical/evidential contract, condensed)

Condensed from the proven contract in the sibling `epistack-casebooks` repo (`kb/lhc/notes/COLLECTION.md`) — full version there; this is illustrative, not the canonical spec.

## Goal

Map a live, sourced disagreement — who asserts what, on what basis — without adjudicating who is right. Quality bar: does this note faithfully represent the state of contestation? Not: is this claim true, or do I believe it?

## Title convention

Topical, never a verdict in the collection's own voice: "Whether the LHC could produce a dangerous micro black hole," not "The LHC cannot produce a dangerous micro black hole."

## Attribution (mandatory)

Every proposition needs:

1. **An attributed asserter** — who holds this position (a name, institution, or paper), kept separate from any judgment about correctness.
2. **A source-span citation** — file link + a prose locator (section/heading) + a grounding-layer marker: *verbatim* (quoted), *paraphrase layer* (source's own summary, not quoted), or *second-hand* (another party's restatement).

## Review

- Title states no verdict in this collection's voice.
- Every substantive claim names its asserter — including synthesis sentences; a nearby citation doesn't count as attribution for a sentence that itself evaluates ("narrows but does not close").
- Every citation carries a prose locator and grounding-layer marker.
- Competing positions get comparable interpretive care — not equal word count, but no side padded with weaker-grade material to look stronger.

## Maintenance

Stale when attribution drifts from what a party now asserts, or a new sourced position emerges — never because a maintainer's own view changed or one side "lost."

## Sources

`sources/` holds short captured excerpts, treated as immutable once written — no elaborate ingest contract here; this is a demonstration, not a production casebook.

## Types

Plain `note` type. No schema — attribution and citation are convention, enforced by this file, not frontmatter.
