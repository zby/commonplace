## Failure mode

A sentence can be read two ways due to its syntactic structure, and the unintended reading is plausible.

## Test

For each sentence, ask: is there a way to read this that means something different from what was intended? Pay special attention to:

- Negation scope: does "not" negate the verb or the object?
- Modifier attachment: which verb does a "when" or "where" clause attach to?
- Pronoun reference: is the antecedent clear?

Only report cases where the unintended reading is plausible, not where it is theoretically possible but no one would actually read it that way.

## Example (fail)

"The mistake is not storing a trace" — reads as "the mistake is [failing to store]" on first pass.

## Example (pass)

"Storing a trace is fine — the mistake is letting stored history automatically become the next call's context."
