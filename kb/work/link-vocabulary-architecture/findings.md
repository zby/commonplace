# Findings — link-label audit

Data source: [`kb/reports/link-vocabulary.md`](../../reports/link-vocabulary.md), generated by [`extract_labels.py`](./extract_labels.py).

## Scale

- 3420 footer-shaped link lines across `kb/`
- 2308 (68%) carry a label-shaped prefix; 1111 (32%) are prose-only
- 32 source/target edges observed; top four account for ~75% of links:
  - `theoretical → theoretical` — 1187
  - `workshop → workshop` — 546
  - `descriptive → theoretical` — 406
  - `descriptive → descriptive` — 376

## How well the declared vocabularies fit

### ADR 009 core survives

On `theoretical → theoretical`, the ADR 009 labels plus `foundation` account for ~35% of the edge:

| label | count | ADR 009? |
|---|---|---|
| `extends` | 138 | yes |
| `foundation` | 128 | yes (alias for `grounds`) |
| `grounds` | 80 | yes |
| `enables` | 42 | yes |
| `exemplifies` | 32 | yes |
| `example` | 9 | yes (alias for `exemplifies`) |
| `contradicts` | 1 | yes |

The only canonical label that isn't working is `contradicts`. One occurrence on the biggest edge is striking — either the KB is genuinely low-conflict, or authors avoid naming conflicts and surface them as `tension` (7) / `challenges` (1) / `counterposition` (1) instead. Worth a prose-level check later: are contradictions being buried as hedged phrases?

### `foundation` and `grounds` are co-dominant aliases

`foundation`: 128 + 27 (workshop) + 18 (descriptive→theoretical) + 15 (managed) + 6 (workshop→theoretical) + 1 (source→theoretical) = **196**
`grounds`: 80 + 45 (workshop) + 18 (descriptive→theoretical) + 23 (source→theoretical) + 4 (descriptive→source) + 7 (managed→theoretical) + 3 (workshop→theoretical) + 1 (managed) + 4 (theoretical→descriptive) + 2 (descriptive→descriptive) = **187**

ADR 009 lists them as equivalent (`grounds` canonical, `foundation` alias). The corpus treats them interchangeably — no clear rule about when each is used. Consider canonicalising on one; the split just doubles the vocabulary surface without adding discrimination.

### Matrix-only additions barely appear in footers

`since`, `because` — 0 occurrences in footer position (they live in inline prose grammar, as the framing predicted).

`qualifies` — 3 occurrences total. `rationale`, `justification`, `derived-from`, `cross-reference`, `see-also`, `supersedes`, `reference`, `composition`, `procedure` — near-zero across the corpus.

The matrix declared richer vocabulary than authors actually use. The collection-topology tables are aspirational; they should either be pruned to match the corpus or have their surplus labels removed.

## Strong off-vocab labels — promotion candidates

Aggregated across all edges, the labels that consistently carry real weight:

| label | total count | likely meaning | action |
|---|---|---|---|
| `contrasts` (+ `contrast`) | **~150** | highlights a difference without claiming conflict | **promote** — no canonical fills this slot |
| `compares` | ~55 | pairwise comparative discussion | **promote** or fold into `contrasts` |
| `sharpens` | ~75 | narrows / refines a claim the target made | **promote** — captures refinement without `extends`'s additive connotation |
| `mechanism` | ~53 | supplies the causal/operational how for a claim | **promote** — distinct from `grounds` (evidential) |
| `applies` | ~40 | uses the target's principle in a new domain | **promote** or fold into `exemplifies` |
| `sibling` | ~29 | same register, parallel treatment | **promote** or fold into `see-also` |
| `complements` / `complement` / `complementary` | ~30 | orthogonal addition, not extension | **promote** or fold into `extends` |
| `consequence` | ~20 | downstream implication of target's claim | fold into `extends` — marginal semantic difference |
| `instance` | ~17 | concrete case | fold into `exemplifies` |
| `motivates` | ~14 | provides the reason for this note | fold into `grounds` — same function, different framing |

Prune candidates (low count, vague, usually fold-able):

- `context`, `related`, `background`, `parallel` — too generic; authors should pick a sharper label or go unlabelled
- `tension` — captures what `contradicts` should; consider explicitly listing as alias
- long tail of singletons (`cost-model`, `formalizes`, `phase-transition`, etc.) — usually local, prose would serve better

## Drift concentrated in machine-generated zones

Two cases where drift is not authorial:

1. **`one-mechanism` (38 in workshop→workshop)** — essentially all from `kb/work/revise-autoreason/` outputs. A mechanical artifact of that skill's prompting, not a signal about vocabulary.
2. **`direct-match`, `candidate`, `rejected` in managed→managed** — structured labels from connect-report generators.

These should be excluded when evaluating the authored corpus. The audit script could filter by path; worth a flag if we rerun.

## Gaps in the declared matrix

The matrix only covers the theoretical/descriptive/prescriptive grid. The corpus uses many edges that have *no* declared vocabulary:

- `theoretical → source` (141) — dominant labels: `exemplifies`, `grounds`, `extends`, `evidence`, `source`, `validates`
- `source → theoretical` (166) — dominant labels: `exemplifies`, `grounds`, `extends`, `validates`
- `workshop → workshop` (546) — dominant labels: `extends`, `grounds`, `exemplifies`, `foundation`, plus the revise-autoreason artefact
- `managed → theoretical` / `managed → managed` — dominant labels: `extends`, `foundation`, `exemplifies`, `sharpens`

The ADR 009 core works across these edges too. Rather than extend the matrix cell-by-cell, the cleanest move is: **ADR 009 labels apply to every authored edge**; per-register outbound tables add only cases where that register has a *genuinely different* relationship vocabulary (e.g. descriptive→descriptive `supersedes` for ADRs).

## Directionality and duality of the vocabulary

Examining each label by how the edge is authored and what the inverse view needs splits the vocabulary cleanly into two classes.

### Asymmetric labels — forward-authored, backward-derived

| label | direction | why no single-label dual is needed |
|---|---|---|
| `extends` | source builds on target | One target extended by many sources; target can't list them all. |
| `grounds` (also `foundation`) | target is source's theoretical / evidential base | One foundation grounds many applications — same density problem. |
| `enables` | target is source's operational prerequisite | One prerequisite enables many dependents. |
| `exemplifies` (also `example`) | source is a concrete instance of target | One general has many instances. |
| `mechanism` | target is source's operational engine | One mechanism underpins many applications. |

Every label in this class points *upstream* (toward more fundamental, more general, or more foundational). Each upstream note typically accumulates many incoming links from downstream users — which is why the upstream note cannot, and should not, maintain forward links back to all of them. The forward edge from the downstream side is the canonical representation; the upstream view is the auto-computed backlinks view.

This is the pattern the user named: "a theory with many systems using it — the systems link up to the theory, the theory would have to link down to too many notes for that to scale." Confirmed as the rule for the whole asymmetric class.

### Symmetric labels — self-dual

| label | direction | dual |
|---|---|---|
| `contradicts` | A conflicts with B ⟺ B conflicts with A | `contradicts` itself |
| `contrasts` | A and B differ on axis X ⟺ same from B's vantage | `contrasts` itself |

These are pair-level relationships with low fan-out. The pairing itself IS the relationship, so both ends may legitimately mark it from their own vantage without creating clutter.

### Correction to an earlier claim

I said `mechanism` and `enables` are duals. They're not — both point *upstream* from the source's end. They're siblings within the asymmetric class, differing in the kind of upstream-ness they name:

- `enables`: target is a prerequisite (more abstract — a condition, resource, permission)
- `mechanism`: target is the operational engine (more specific — the actual machinery)

Neither has a single-label ADR 009 dual, and neither needs one. The symmetry I imagined was wrong.

### Implications for ADR 018

1. **Add `contrasts` and `mechanism` as corpus-validated promotions.** Both fit cleanly: `contrasts` into the symmetric class (self-dual peer-level), `mechanism` into the asymmetric class (upstream-pointing, same shape as `extends`/`grounds`/`enables`/`exemplifies`).
2. **State the directional asymmetry principle explicitly.** ADR 009 leaves it implicit, but it's load-bearing. Without it, future label proposals drift toward "every forward needs a paired backward" — which doesn't match the fan-out structure.
3. **Allow curated selective back-links.** An upstream note *may* link down to index a canonical application or pin a key example. This is editorial curation, not inverse-duty.
4. **Register discipline for placement in `COLLECTION.md`.** From earlier analysis: `grounds` is a cross-register upward move (appears on almost every outbound edge that can reach theoretical); `mechanism` is intra-theoretical only (add only to the `theoretical → theoretical` row, not scattered).

## Tie-back to linking theory

[`linking-theory.md`](../../notes/linking-theory.md) predicts a label earns its place if it lets an agent decide follow/skip differently than an adjacent label would. The corpus shape is consistent with that:

- The ADR 009 core is used heavily and broadly → it discriminates decisions.
- `since`/`because` aren't used in footers → they're grammar connectors, not decision cues; the matrix was wrong to list them as footer labels.
- `contrasts`, `sharpens`, `mechanism` recur with consistent semantics → authors needed discriminations the five-label set didn't cover.
- Unlabelled prose at 32% → authors sometimes decline to commit a label; worth sampling to see whether the prose itself carries the signal.

The open question linking-theory flagged — *"Is the vocabulary the right one?"* — has a partial answer: the ADR 009 core is roughly right but under-covers; a vocabulary of ~10 labels (current 5 + `contrasts`, `sharpens`, `mechanism`, `applies` or similar) probably matches observed decision-making better.

## Proposed decision (iterated)

Initial pass suggested promoting a large set (`contrasts`, `sharpens`, `mechanism`, `applies`, `compares`, `complements`, …). On closer reading most of those fold into existing ADR 009 labels or aliases:

- `sharpens`, `refines`, `clarifies` → `extends` already covers "builds on, adds a dimension to, **refines**"
- `motivates`, `rationale`, `justification` → `grounds`
- `applies`, `instance`, `application` → `exemplifies`
- `complements`, `consequence`, `sibling` → `extends` or `see-also`
- `tension`, `challenges` → `contradicts`

Only two candidates survive with genuine semantic daylight:

1. **`contrasts`** — distinct from `contradicts`. Used by authors to name a difference between peers without claiming conflict. Review-register uses it heavily (descriptive→descriptive 47, descriptive→theoretical 77) to map similar-but-different systems.
2. **`mechanism`** — distinct from `grounds`. Operational machinery ("by what operation?") vs epistemic base ("on what premises?"). Register pattern differs too: `grounds` crosses registers upward, `mechanism` stays within theoretical.

**Plan:**

1. Write ADR 018 (extends ADR 009; does not supersede) that (a) promotes `contrasts` and `mechanism`, (b) documents the directional-asymmetry principle explicitly, and (c) records the folds above so the canonical vocabulary does not drift.
2. Update the relevant COLLECTION.md outbound tables:
   - `kb/agent-memory-systems/COLLECTION.md` — add `contrasts` to descriptive→descriptive and descriptive→theoretical rows.
   - `kb/notes/COLLECTION.md` — add `contrasts` to theoretical→theoretical; add `mechanism` to theoretical→theoretical.
   - `kb/reference/COLLECTION.md` — add `contrasts` to descriptive→descriptive (ADR cross-refs).
3. Also strip the outbound tables of labels that don't appear in practice as footer labels (`since`/`because` — they're inline grammar; `rationale`/`justification`/`qualifies`/`derived-from` — near-zero corpus use).
4. Run `cp-skill-compile-collections` to refresh `kb/reports/collection-topology.md`.
5. Rerun the audit after edits to measure convergence on the canonical vocabulary.
