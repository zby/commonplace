# Ranking by authority: identity is a field, track record is a dossier, rank is derived

Layer: assessment (with one ingestion-layer field).

Ranking sources by authority has two entry points — genre and author. Genre is on its way to being a checkable open-vocabulary field (see the framework-side [source-genre proposal](../../reference/proposals/source-genre-is-one-open-field-on-the-snapshot.md)); the author entry point needs its own decomposition, because "how much weight does this author carry" is exactly the kind of judgment the assessment-layer doctrines forbid storing. The objective/checkable vs judgment/contextual split (developed in the [extensible-controlled-vocabularies workshop](../extensible-controlled-vocabularies/README.md), "epistemic strength is not a storable scalar") factors authority into three layers:

1. **Identity facts are capture fields.** Who wrote it, and under what institution, is objective — [a richer source type](./richer-source-type.md) already names author/institution as first-class capture metadata, on the strength of [history has one chance to become checkable](../../notes/history-has-one-chance-to-become-checkable.md). The field sits on the snapshot next to genre and gets the same open-vocabulary treatment. It pays for itself twice: author identity is also a shared-dependency key for [independence clustering](./independence-clustering.md) — shared authorship is one of the correlations that turn ten papers into three lines of evidence.

2. **Track record is an entity dossier, attributed entry by entry.** Authors recur across sources, so what is known about one (affiliation history, prior predictions and their outcomes, retractions, conflicts of interest, funding) lives once, in a per-author artifact that snapshots reference by identity — not smeared across the snapshots themselves. Every dossier entry is a sourced, attributed statement, per [confidence must be attributed, never a mark](./attributed-confidence-not-marks.md): the dossier holds facts about the author and attributed judgments of the author, never a credibility scalar. `authority: high` on an author artifact is the same forbidden non-recomputable mark as `confidence: 0.7` on a claim, one entity up. [Party/position attribution](./party-position-attribution.md) already needs recurring attributed actors (LSAG, Wagner and Sancho); a party and an author are the same shape — a recurring actor other artifacts attribute things to — so one entity mechanism should be designed to serve both before two grow independently.

3. **Rank is derived, downstream, replaceable.** An authority ranking is an adjudication output, per [adjudication as a separate, labeled, downstream layer](./adjudication-as-separate-layer.md): computed over genre + dossier + independence clusters by a dashboard or verdict artifact ([dashboards and source-impact](./dashboards-and-source-impact.md)), labeled as opinion, and never written back into snapshot or note frontmatter. The ranking is the most volatile and most contestable object in the whole system, so it must stay the cheapest to discard — the mirror argument to the dossier being durable because its entries are checkable.

The genuinely new machinery this candidate proposes is only layer 2 — an author/party entity artifact. Its new cost is identity resolution: name variants, shared names, institutional vs personal authorship. Casebook-first experiment: an `authors/` directory in `epistack-casebooks` with one plain markdown dossier per *recurring* author (skip single-appearance authors), referenced by filename from snapshots' author fields; see whether filename-as-identity survives real casework before designing registries, aliases, or schemas. Layers 1 and 3 need no new design — they are [richer-source-type](./richer-source-type.md) and [dashboards-and-source-impact](./dashboards-and-source-impact.md) doing their existing jobs with one more input.

Failure mode to watch in the experiment: dossiers drifting from attributed track-record entries into editorial character judgments ("unreliable", "crank") — that is the adjudication layer leaking upstream into the map, the exact collapse [rejected for the case root](./rejected-candidates.md).
