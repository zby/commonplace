# Sentence-Level Review (experiment)

**Target: kb/work/review-revise/baseline.md**

## What this is

A read-only review that reads sentence by sentence, looking for local defects. The existing prose review checks for note-level representational patterns (source residue, proportion mismatch, etc.). This review checks whether individual sentences say what they mean.

Report findings as WARN (likely misleads the reader) or INFO (mildly unclear), never FAIL.

## Prerequisites

Read the target note in full. Note: this note originally lived at `kb/notes/session-history-should-not-be-the-default-next-context.md`. Resolve links relative to `kb/notes/` when checking link targets.

## Checks

### 1. Parsing ambiguity

**Failure mode:** A sentence can be read two ways due to its syntactic structure, and the unintended reading is plausible.

**Test:** For each sentence, ask: is there a way to read this that means something different from what was intended? Pay special attention to:
- Negation scope: "The mistake is not storing a trace" — does "not" negate "mistake" or "storing"?
- Modifier attachment: "raw history is the easiest way to preserve maximum information when the caller does not yet know what matters" — does "when" attach to "preserve" or to "easiest"?
- Pronoun reference: "it preserves everything" — is the antecedent clear?

Only report cases where the unintended reading is plausible, not where it is theoretically possible but no one would actually read it that way.

### 2. Framing mismatch

**Failure mode:** A sentence frames its claim in terms of X, but the actual mechanism is Y. The framing narrows or misdirects the reader's understanding.

**Test:** For each sentence that gives a reason or explains a mechanism, ask: is the stated framing the right level of generality? Is the mechanism correctly identified?

**Examples of failure:** "For orchestration, that is usually the wrong trade" — frames the problem as orchestration-specific, but the actual mechanism (LLMs degrade with context complexity) applies to any call needing full cognitive capacity.

**Examples of passing:** "LLMs degrade with context complexity — every token spent parsing irrelevant history is cognitive budget not spent on the actual task" — frames the problem at the right level.

### 3. Stock phrases

**Failure mode:** A sentence uses a pattern commonly generated by LLMs that adds no content. These patterns sound authoritative but are semantically empty.

**Test:** Delete the sentence. If the surrounding text loses no information or argumentative force, the sentence was filler.

**Common patterns to check for:**
- "This is not just X — it is Y" (the "elevation" move)
- "It's worth noting that..." / "It's important to note that..."
- "In other words, ..." followed by a near-verbatim restatement
- "This has profound implications for..."
- "At its core, ..."
- "The key insight here is that..."

Not every instance of these patterns is bad — flag only when the sentence adds nothing that the surrounding text doesn't already establish.

### 4. Misleading link text

**Failure mode:** The text of a markdown link implies a concept, but the linked note discusses something different. The reader who follows the link will be confused; the reader who doesn't will have a wrong impression of what the link supports.

**Test:** For each markdown link, read the link text and the sentence it appears in. What does the reader expect to find at the target? Then read the target note's title and opening paragraph (resolve relative to `kb/notes/`). Does the target match the expectation set by the link text?

**Example of failure:** "This is the [return-value problem](./llm-context-is-composed-without-scoping.md) in architectural form" — link text says "return-value problem" but the target is about context scoping; its "return value problem" section discusses interface typing, not trace leakage.

## Output

Write the review to `kb/work/review-revise/run-01/sentence-review.md` using this format:

```
=== SENTENCE-LEVEL REVIEW: baseline.md ===

Checks applied: 4

WARN:
- [{check-name}] {finding with specific quote from the note}
  Recommendation: {what to change}

INFO:
- [{check-name}] {finding}

CLEAN:
- [{check-name}] {what was checked and why it held}

Overall: {CLEAN / {N} warnings, {M} info}
===
```

Report every check — WARN, INFO, or CLEAN.

## Do NOT

- Do not modify the target note. This is read-only analysis.
- Do not report note-level patterns (proportion, structure, completeness). Those belong to other reviews.
- Do not flag every instance of a stock phrase — only flag when the sentence adds nothing.
- Do not follow more than 5 links for the misleading-link-text check.
