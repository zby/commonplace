=== PROSE REVIEW: context-efficiency-is-the-central-design-concern-in-agent-systems.md ===

Checks applied: 8

WARN:
- [Confidence miscalibration] "Context is the scarce resource in agent systems — not compute, memory, or storage" and "context is the lowest-degree-of-freedom resource in agent systems" are presented as established facts, but this is the note's own framing, not a cited finding. The Anthropic source calls context "a critical but finite resource," which is weaker than "the only fundamental scarce resource." The note escalates a practitioner heuristic to a universal claim without flagging the gap. The closing sentence — "If context is the only fundamental scarce resource" — at least uses a conditional, but the opening paragraph and description assert it unconditionally.
  Recommendation: Hedge the opening claim to match its epistemic status: something like "In current agent architectures, context functions as the binding scarce resource" rather than asserting it as a permanent, universal truth. Alternatively, add a caveat acknowledging that this is a design-era observation that may shift as architectures evolve (e.g., retrieval-augmented systems with external memory tiers partially relax the unitary-channel claim).

- [Proportion mismatch] The core claim is that context efficiency is the *central design concern* — meaning it should dominate architectural decisions. The "Architectural responses" section is where that claim earns its weight, but it is a bullet list with one-line descriptions. Meanwhile, "Prior work" and "Volume and complexity" get full paragraph treatment for what are essentially setup sections. The most architecturally substantive content (frontloading, progressive disclosure, sub-agent isolation, navigation design) is compressed into link-heavy bullets that delegate almost all substance to other notes. The result is that the note reads more as an index of architectural responses than as an argument for why context efficiency is central.
  Recommendation: Either develop 2-3 of the architectural responses with enough inline reasoning that a reader sees the argument without following links, or retitle the note to match what it actually delivers (closer to "context scarcity drives architectural patterns in agent systems" — a catalog claim rather than a centrality claim).

- [Unbridged cross-domain evidence] The "Prior work" section cites Simon (1971) on human attention, Miller (1956) and Cowan (2001) on human working memory, and Toffler (1970) on human information overload. The bridging sentence — "What's specific to agent systems is the unitary channel, the hard token limit, and the interaction between volume and complexity" — identifies differences but does not explain the shared mechanism that makes the human findings relevant. Why does human attention economics transfer to token-based context windows? The analogy is plausible but the note treats it as self-evident.
  Recommendation: Add a bridge sentence articulating the shared mechanism: both human working memory and LLM context windows are fixed-capacity buffers where all content competes for influence on the next output, making capacity-management findings transferable at the structural level even though the mechanisms differ.

INFO:
- [Source residue] The note is pitched at a general "agent systems" level. Most of the practitioner evidence comes from coding agents specifically — Lopopolo's Codex codebase, Koylan's Personal Brain OS for coding workflows, the JetBrains research on coding agents. The note does not frame these as domain-specific examples; they are cited as general evidence. This is not clearly wrong (coding agents are the most mature agent domain), but a reader from a different agent domain (customer service, research, data analysis) might find the evidence base narrower than the claim scope suggests.
  Recommendation: Consider a brief acknowledgment that the practitioner evidence is predominantly from coding agents, with a note on why the findings are expected to generalize (or flagging that as an open question).

- [Redundant restatement] The opening of "Volume and complexity" — "The soft bound operates across two dimensions — volume (how many tokens) and complexity (how hard they are to use) — decomposed in [agent context is constrained...]" — restates what the third paragraph of the introduction already established: "The binding constraint is soft degradation, not hard token limits — established in [agent context is constrained...]." Both paragraphs point to the same note and re-establish the same premise. The section then adds new content (Koylan evidence, the "not fully separable" point), but the opening sentence is pure restatement.
  Recommendation: Cut or compress the opening of "Volume and complexity" to one transitional clause and lead with the new content (the distinguishable-but-not-separable point or the Koylan evidence).

CLEAN:
- [Pseudo-formalism] No formal notation, equations, or mathematical apparatus. The note uses prose throughout. The phrase "lowest-degree-of-freedom resource" could be read as pseudo-formal, but it links to a note that defines the concept, so it functions as a term of art rather than decorative formalism. Clean.

- [Orphan references] All specific claims are sourced. "Roughly 30x per year since mid-2023" cites Epoch AI (2025). The Koylan "40% token reduction" cites the source. The Anthropic quotes are attributed. The "Prior work" citations (Simon, Miller, Cowan, Toffler) are named with years — these are well-known enough that full citations are not required in a note, though they are not linked. No unsupported specific numbers found.

- [Anthropomorphic framing] The note consistently uses precise language: "the model degrades," "the agent receives instructions," "the agent can attend to." No instances of "understands," "knows," "believes," or "possesses knowledge." The phrase "understands its task" appears once in the opening paragraph — "the finite window of tokens the agent can attend to... understands its task" — but in context this is describing the functional role of the context window (it is the channel through which the agent *processes* its task), not making a cognitive claim. Borderline but defensible.

Overall: 3 warnings, 2 info
