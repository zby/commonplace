WARN:
- [Completeness] The note claims "two distinct ways" that structured templates constrain LLM generation (process structure and output structure), framing these as exhaustive. A boundary case challenges this: **cognitive load / attention allocation structure** — constraints on what the model should attend to or ignore (e.g., "focus only on the error-handling paths," "ignore variable names"). This is neither output structure (it does not constrain the shape of the result) nor process structure as defined (it does not prescribe reasoning steps); it constrains the scope of attention. The note's own peer-review-checklist example ("did the authors address confounds?") could be read as an attention-directing constraint rather than a process-step constraint — it tells you what to look for, not what reasoning steps to perform. The two-item enumeration may be incomplete, or the definitions need tightening to clearly absorb attention-directing constraints into one of the two categories.

- [Grounding alignment] The note attributes the 5-12pp accuracy gain primarily to process constraints: "instructions like 'must state premises' and 'must trace paths' force specific reasoning work that a heading-only constraint would not." But the note itself acknowledges (claim 7) that the paper "does not ablate individual template components, so it cannot isolate how much of the gain comes from process constraints versus the incidental output formatting." The note's framing makes the locus of effect sound more settled than the evidence supports. The phrase "makes the locus of effect visible" implies the paper demonstrates the mechanism, when actually the note is making a design-reading inference from the template structure, not citing an empirical result. The inference is reasonable but presented with more confidence than the evidence warrants.

- [Grounding alignment — domain coverage] The note's central claim is that process structure and output structure are independent levers for constraining LLM generation in general. The sole empirical source (Ugare & Chandra) covers code verification tasks only. The note generalises freely to "LLM generation" broadly, and the human-methodology examples (peer review, style guides, scientific papers) are illustrative rather than evidential. The claim domain (all structured LLM generation) exceeds the source domain (code verification with semi-formal templates). The note's seedling status partially mitigates this, but the gap is worth flagging.

INFO:
- [Completeness] The independence claim is demonstrated via four examples but tested only at the extremes (pure process, pure output, both). A boundary case worth considering: **few-shot examples**. A few-shot example constrains both process and output simultaneously and inseparably — the model infers both the expected reasoning pattern and the expected output format from the same exemplar. If the two dimensions are truly independent, it should be possible to provide few-shot examples that constrain only process or only output. Whether this is achievable in practice is non-obvious and could stress the independence claim. The note does not address mixed-constraint mechanisms where the two dimensions cannot be separately controlled.

- [Completeness] The note identifies two mechanisms (distribution selection and interpretation narrowing) that "apply differently" to each structure type. A third mechanism in the KB — failure-mode transfer (from human-writing-structures-transfer-to-llms-because-failure-modes-overlap.md) — is referenced in the Relevant Notes but not analysed through the process/output lens. If the note claims to show how "the KB's two main explanatory mechanisms" split across the two structure types, the omission of failure-mode transfer is consistent with that framing but leaves an obvious follow-up unaddressed.

- [Internal consistency] The note says "The two dimensions are independent" (claim 4) and later says they "draw from overlapping but distinct subsets of the training distribution" (claim 8). These are consistent — independence of the levers does not require independence of their effects. But a reader could misread "independent levers" as implying fully separable effects. The note could be clearer that "independent" refers to the design dimensions (you can set one without the other), not to the downstream causal pathways (which overlap).

- [Internal consistency] The note's description field says "the agentic-code-reasoning evidence shows process constraints driving accuracy gains where output format alone would not." This is stronger than the body, which carefully notes the lack of ablation (claim 7). The description presents the conclusion the body hedges. This is the summary-elides-tension pattern the review procedure flags.
