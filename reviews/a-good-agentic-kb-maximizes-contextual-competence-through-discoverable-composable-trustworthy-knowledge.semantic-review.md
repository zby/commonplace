=== SEMANTIC REVIEW: a-good-agentic-kb-maximizes-contextual-competence-through-discoverable-composable-trustworthy-knowledge.md ===

Claims identified: 18

WARN:
- [Completeness] The three properties (discoverable, composable, trustworthy) claim to be what knowledge needs "for knowledge to improve an agent's actions under bounded context." Boundary case: **timeliness/currency**. The note itself acknowledges that "a reviewed, validated, `status: current` note can become wrong because the world changed" and calls currency "a maintenance concern that the status ladder signals but doesn't enforce." But currency is not merely a maintenance detail — stale knowledge that passes all three property checks (it is findable, it composes with other notes, it was once trustworthy) actively degrades competence. The note treats currency as a sub-concern of trustworthiness, but a stale note that still *looks* trustworthy (all structural signals intact) is the most dangerous failure mode. This suggests either trustworthiness needs to be defined more broadly (to explicitly include currency) or currency is a fourth property. The note notices this gap but classifies it as out of scope rather than resolving it.

- [Completeness] The dependency structure — "Discoverability is the foundation... Trustworthiness depends on discoverability... Composability depends on the other two" — claims a linear dependency chain. Boundary case: **a trustworthy note that is hard to discover.** A well-structured, validated, correct note buried in the wrong directory with a poor description is trustworthy but not discoverable. Its trustworthiness exists independently of whether anyone can find it. The note's claim that "a note that can't be found can't be challenged, corrected, or kept current" conflates the *maintenance* of trustworthiness with trustworthiness itself. A note's claims are either well-grounded or they aren't, regardless of discoverability. The dependency may hold for *sustained* trustworthiness over time but not for trustworthiness as an intrinsic property of the artifact.

- [Grounding — domain coverage] The note cites [constraining-and-distillation-both-trade-generality-for-compound] to ground the claim that constraining and distillation "trade generality for the reliability+speed+cost compound." The source note explicitly covers constraining and distillation as the two mechanisms that make this trade-off. However, the reviewed note adds **pruning** and **discovery** as third and fourth operations — neither appears in the source. The source's two-mechanism framework is silently promoted to a four-operation (plus accumulation) framework. Readers following the link may expect the source to ground the full operation set, but it only grounds two of the four transformation operations.

INFO:
- [Completeness] The five operations (accumulation, constraining, distillation, discovery, pruning) claim to cover how a KB "improves through learning." Boundary case: **reorganization/refactoring** — changing the structure of existing knowledge (splitting a note, merging two notes, moving a note to a different area, renaming for better discoverability) without constraining, distilling, discovering, or pruning it. Reorganization changes the *arrangement* without changing the *content*. It could arguably be absorbed into "distillation" (compression) or treated as a sub-operation of several. But a pure structural refactor (renaming a file for better discoverability, fixing a tag) is none of the five — it is maintenance of the properties rather than a learning operation. This may be intentional scoping (the note is about learning operations, not maintenance operations), but the boundary is worth flagging because the note claims "all five must operate continuously" as if they exhaust what the KB needs.

- [Completeness] Boundary case for the three properties: **actionability** — knowledge that is discoverable, composable, and trustworthy but does not actually help the agent decide what to do. A note that accurately explains why something is the case, composes with other notes, and is easy to find may still leave the agent unable to act because it lacks a prescriptive element. The note's own "Tension: reach vs action value" section partially addresses this by noting that "an action-oriented system needs preferences, procedures, and judgment precedents — knowledge types that are inherently low-reach." But actionability as a property of individual notes (does this note help me act?) is not quite captured by any of the three properties, which are about findability, combinability, and reliability.

- [Grounding — vocabulary] The note says "Distillation primarily improves **discoverability**: a distilled skill fits in one context load where fifteen methodology notes wouldn't." The source note [constraining-and-distillation-both-trade-generality-for-compound] describes distillation as producing compound gains in "speed, cost" and "reliability" — it does not use the word "discoverability" or frame distillation's primary contribution as improving discoverability. The reviewed note's mapping of distillation → discoverability is the note's own theoretical move, not a direct attribution from the source. The inference is reasonable (fitting in one context load does improve practical findability), but the source frames the benefit differently (as compound gain, not as a property improvement).

- [Grounding — inference] The note claims "Constraining preserves reach but doesn't create it. A codified script is no more explanatory than the prompt it replaced — just more reliable." The source [first-principles-reasoning-selects-for-explanatory-reach-over-adaptive-fit] discusses reach as a property of explanatory knowledge but does not explicitly address whether constraining preserves or destroys reach. The claim is plausible (constraining changes the medium, not the explanation), but it is the note's own inference, not grounded in the cited source.

- [Internal consistency] The compressed summary says "Four operations transform accumulated knowledge: constraining improves trustworthiness, distillation improves discoverability, discovery improves composability and produces the highest-reach items to accumulate, and pruning removes stale knowledge before it erodes trust." This accurately reflects the body but omits the secondary property improvements that the body carefully enumerates (constraining secondarily improves discoverability, distillation secondarily improves composability, etc.). The compression is faithful to the primary mappings but loses the cross-cutting effects, which slightly overstates the clean one-operation-one-property mapping.

PASS:

Attribution is accurate.

The source accurately covers both: "Adaptive information — structures that help a system cope with the world" vs "Explanatory knowledge — says why the world works a certain way" with "The distinguishing property is reach." Attribution is accurate.

The source explicitly introduces this term: "The common thread isn't question-answering — it's contextual competence: the ability to act appropriately given accumulated knowledge about the domain, the user, and the project." Attribution is accurate.

- [Internal consistency] The body uses "three properties" and "four operations" (plus accumulation) consistently throughout. The table in the operations section correctly maps all five. The "Reach" section correctly cross-references the operations with reach effects. No contradictions found between sections.

- [Internal consistency] The note's treatment of the reach-vs-action-value tension is internally consistent — it acknowledges the tension, gives two reasons it is "real but not a contradiction," and does not claim to fully resolve it. The body and summary are aligned on this point.
