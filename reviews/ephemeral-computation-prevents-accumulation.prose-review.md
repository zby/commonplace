Checks applied: 8

WARN:
- [Confidence miscalibration] "Ephemeral computation is the deliberate refusal to codify. It stays permanently in the stochastic regime, re-deriving solutions each time." This frames the note's own conceptual construction — positioning ephemerality as the anti-codification endpoint — as established fact. The ephemeral/codification spectrum is the note's own model, yet the language asserts it without hedging ("is the deliberate refusal," "stays permanently"). More precisely, calling ephemerality a "deliberate refusal" attributes intentionality to a system design choice that may simply be an unconsidered default — many ephemeral systems are ephemeral not by deliberate refusal but because persistence was never built. The framing loads a claim about design intent that the note does not defend.
  Recommendation: Flag this as the note's proposed framing. E.g., "Ephemeral computation can be understood as the inverse of codification — it remains in the stochastic regime, re-deriving solutions each time." If the "deliberate" qualifier is important, add a sentence justifying why ephemerality in RLM-type systems reflects an intentional design choice rather than an omission.

- [Source residue] The note claims general scope — its title addresses "ephemeral computation" and "accumulation" without domain restriction — but the only concrete system named is "Recursive Language Models (RLMs)." The fork section introduces RLMs as though they are the canonical example of ephemeral systems, yet RLMs are a niche research construct not widely known. The note never names another ephemeral system (serverless functions, shell pipelines, Jupyter notebooks used without saving, etc.), which makes the general framing lean heavily on a single domain-specific reference. The RLM framing also leaks into the conceptual structure: "the LLM writes code, the REPL executes it, the result feeds back into the conversation, and the code disappears" is an RLM-specific workflow presented as though it typifies all ephemeral computation.
  Recommendation: Either add one or two non-RLM examples of ephemeral computation to anchor the general claim, or frame the RLM description explicitly as one instance ("For example, Recursive Language Models work this way...") rather than letting it serve as the sole illustration of the category.
