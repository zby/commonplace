## Most common findings

### 1. Completeness/boundary cases (275 warnings)

The dominant finding across the KB. Notes introduce enumerations, taxonomies, and frameworks that claim to cover a space but have boundary cases that fall outside all enumerated items. The most common patterns:

- **Missing enumeration items**: Frameworks that list N items but have N+1 real cases — often the omitted item is one the note's own linked sources discuss
- **Boundary cases between categories**: Items that straddle two enumerated categories, revealing that the partition is not clean
- **Degenerate/extreme cases**: The simplest or most extreme instance of a concept that breaks the framework's assumptions

Examples:
- [agent-is-a-tool-loop](../kb/notes/agent-is-a-tool-loop.md): Three-component definition omits state management
- [automating-kb-learning-is-an-open-problem](../kb/notes/automating-kb-learning-is-an-open-problem.md): Seven-mutation enumeration omits Merge and Verify
- [always-loaded-context-has-two-surfaces-with-different-affordances](../kb/notes/always-loaded-context-has-two-surfaces-with-different-affordances.md): Two surfaces may undercount — memory files and routing instructions don't fit either

### 2. Grounding alignment (164 warnings)

The second most common finding. Notes cite linked sources but the relationship between what the source says and what the note claims is not as tight as the link implies. Common patterns:

- **Scope mismatch**: The note's claim domain exceeds the source's domain — the source grounds a subset of the claim but readers may assume full coverage
- **Vocabulary mismatch**: The note uses terms the source doesn't, presenting the note's own inference as attribution
- **Interpretive overlay**: The note applies its own analytical framework to the source and presents the result as what the source says

Examples:
- [context-efficiency-is-the-central-design-concern-in-agent-systems](../kb/notes/context-efficiency-is-the-central-design-concern-in-agent-systems.md): Lopopolo source says bottleneck was 'the environment', note narrows to 'context structure'
- [codification-and-relaxing-navigate-the-bitter-lesson-boundary](../kb/notes/codification-and-relaxing-navigate-the-bitter-lesson-boundary.md): Ebrahimi scaling claim exceeds the source's scope (small fixed-size models only)
- [alexander-patterns-and-knowledge-system-design](../kb/notes/alexander-patterns-and-knowledge-system-design.md): Link relationship types attributed to link-contracts note don't match that note's actual taxonomy

### 3. Internal consistency (8 warnings)

The least common but often most actionable. Notes where different sections make claims that are in tension with each other. Examples:

- [deep-search-is-connection-methodology-applied-to-temporarily-expanded-corpus](../kb/notes/deep-search-is-connection-methodology-applied-to-temporarily-expanded-corpus.md): Title claims no new connection logic needed, but Phase 3 introduces synthesis and gap detection not in /connect
- [stale-indexes-are-worse-than-no-indexes](../kb/notes/stale-indexes-are-worse-than-no-indexes.md): Defenses section describes superseded infrastructure (areas: field, docs/indexes.md)
