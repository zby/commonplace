## Most common findings

### 1. Confidence miscalibration (132 warnings)

The most pervasive finding across the KB. Notes consistently present their own proposed frameworks, taxonomies, and analytical constructions using assertive language ("requires," "consists of," "the three stages are") rather than hedged framing ("a plausible decomposition," "one way to model this"). This is especially common in notes that introduce original multi-part decompositions or mapping tables. Examples:

- [context-efficiency-is-the-central-design-concern-in-agent-systems](../kb/notes/context-efficiency-is-the-central-design-concern-in-agent-systems.md): "Context is the scarce resource" asserted as fact
- [bounded-context-orchestration-model](../kb/notes/bounded-context-orchestration-model.md): Own proposed framework presented with assertive framing
- [agent-runtimes-decompose-into-scheduler-context-engine-and-execution-substrate](../kb/notes/agent-runtimes-decompose-into-scheduler-context-engine-and-execution-substrate.md): Three-part decomposition asserted as discovered convergence

### 2. Proportion mismatch (90 warnings)

The second most common finding. Notes frequently give thin treatment to their core claim (usually the title) while developing supporting or secondary ideas at greater length. This typically happens when a secondary idea was easier to write or was developed first. The pattern is especially visible in notes where the core claim gets one paragraph while examples, mappings, or subsidiary frameworks get multiple sections. Examples:

- [active-campaign-understanding-needs-a-single-coherent-narrative-not-composed-notes](../kb/notes/active-campaign-understanding-needs-a-single-coherent-narrative-not-composed-notes.md): Core claim in one paragraph, theorist evidence gets twice the space
- [constraining-and-distillation-both-trade-generality-for-reliability-speed-and-cost](../kb/notes/constraining-and-distillation-both-trade-generality-for-reliability-speed-and-cost.md): Title claims parity but constraining gets 2x the development
- [decomposition-rules-for-bounded-context-scheduling](../kb/notes/decomposition-rules-for-bounded-context-scheduling.md): Rules section is terse imperatives while motivational sections are longer

### 3. Source residue (25 warnings)

Notes generalized from domain-specific sources retain concrete framing from those sources — stale directory paths (`docs/notes/` instead of `kb/notes/`, `claw-design/` references), tool-specific examples treated as the referent rather than illustrations, and vocabulary that assumes a narrower domain than the note claims. Examples:

- [001-generate-topic-links-from-frontmatter](../kb/notes/001-generate-topic-links-from-frontmatter.md): References `docs/notes/` and `docs/claw-design/` — paths that don't exist in this repo
